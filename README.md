# brain-simulation
Making a digital simulation of a biological brain and training it with large datasets and making your machine "THINK" and actually achieve partial consciousness.

My insights;

Literally the next step to AI models are, near-perfectly simulated brain neurons (Near-perfectly because only spikes of neurons and neuron connectivity can be simulated, not proteins and organnels inside a neuron cell). 

Literal thinking brain LLMs.

Hala Point proved this model works by simulating 1.15 Billion neurons.
Hala Point runned a Neuromorphic LLM and it did most excellent thing you can with an LLM, It learned itself on-point with real-time data streams. Do you realize how groundbreaking this is for normal LLMs?

To simulate a 86 billion neuron digital brain, we need a 86 billion neuron chip.

We need 100 trillion of artifical synapses (memristors) storing brain(simulated)'s connection weights(spikes) and update them continously in real-time.

Memristors work by moving atoms inside a material changing its electrical resistance. Every time chip does learning, memristor wears out. Like bending a paper billion times.

We need synapses that can endure about 10¹⁵ (one quadrillion) times bending. 
We do not have a material working that can handle this many shifts.
NbO2 is the current new most promising material because it can handle much much more cycles than HfO2(currently used material).

NbO₂ is currently stuck in the laboratory phase.
